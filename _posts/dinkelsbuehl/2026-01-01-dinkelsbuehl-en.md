## History of Dinkelsbühl
Dinkelsbühl is the third German town that still retains its complete medieval city walls, situated between Rothenburg ob der Tauber and Nördlingen. While it may not be as famous as the other two cities, Dinkelsbühl boasts nearly a thousand years of history and served as a vital commercial hub during the Middle Ages.

Settlements were known to exist here as early as the 10th century, with the earliest documentary record dating back to 1188. In that year, the Holy Roman Emperor Frederick I Barbarossa gifted Dinkelsbühl—then known as "burgum tinkelspuhel"—to his son, Conrad II, Duke of Swabia, as a wedding present. Fortunately, Dinkelsbühl did not suffer significant destruction in subsequent wars, allowing us to still admire its well-preserved medieval architecture today.

According to statistics, there are nearly 780 old houses in the historic center of Dinkelsbühl protected as European cultural heritage sites. Approximately 44% of these buildings date back to around 1500. For those who love medieval charm, this is an excellent destination to explore.
