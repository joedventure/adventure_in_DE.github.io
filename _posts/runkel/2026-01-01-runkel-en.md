## Burg Runkel
![Burg Runkel]({{ site.baseurl }}/_image/Runkel/Runkel.jpg)
According to legend, a knight of Charlemagne decided in 778 to build a castle on the rocky banks of the Lahn River to commemorate the battle against the Moors at Roncesvalles in the Pyrenees. Originally named Ronkeval, the name eventually evolved into Runkel.

In historical records, Runkel was first mentioned in 1159. In 1427, the lord Dietrich IV von Runkel acquired the County of Wied through his marriage to Anastasia von Isenburg-Wied, and his descendants took the name Wied. Over the following centuries, Burg Runkel was continuously expanded until October 15, 1634, during the Thirty Years' War, when the Croatian troops of Count Isolani (Johann Ludwig Hektor Graf von Isolani) breached the walls and burned it down. Since then, the main or upper castle (Kernburg) has not been rebuilt; only the lower castle was restored or further expanded.

To this day, Burg Runkel remains the property of the House of Wied.
